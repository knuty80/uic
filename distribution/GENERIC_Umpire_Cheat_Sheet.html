Umpire Field Guide

Tactical Cheat Sheet

Be Brief. Loud. Assertive.

Plate Positioning & Mechanics

  • The "Slot": Position yourself in the space between the catcher and the batter.
  • Locked Position: Get set as the pitcher prepares. Hands behind the catcher to protect them.
  • Clearing the Catcher: Once the ball is hit, "open the gate" by stepping back/out to let the catcher move. Follow the play toward 1st if needed.

Base Positioning (60ft Diamond)

The Outside Theory: In Little League (where no leading off is allowed), base umpires start outside the infield.

  • Pos A (No Runners): Foul territory, 10-15ft behind 1st.
  • Pos B & C (Runners On): Outfield grass behind infielders.
    • B: Behind 2nd baseman.
    • C: Behind shortstop.
  • The "Library": Working area inside diamond. Move here only after ball is in play.

Plate Meeting Checklist

  • League: [League Name] ([ID])
  • Equipment: Mgrs confirmed inspected.
  • Clock: Plate watch is OFFICIAL. State start time loudly.
  • Roll-back: Final score rolls back? Yes/No
  • Boundaries: Define "Imaginary Lines" for unfenced fields.
  • Dugout: All equipment inside. "Clarifications only—no judgment."

Complex Scenarios

1st to 3rd: Plate ump covers 3rd on base hit with R1. Coordinate with signals.
Timing Plays: Plate initiates signal; Base must echo.
Rundowns: Split the field. Eye contact! Shout "Partner, I’ve got this—Out!"

Verbal & Physical Signals

Situation Verbal Physical Action
Strike "Strike!" Right hand forward (pound wall).
Ball "Ball!" None. Use LH fingers for count.
Full Count "Full Count!" 3 fingers LH / 2 fingers RH (No fist bumps).
Outs "Out!" Fist pump or point.
Safe "Safe!" Arms spread wide.
No Tag "No tag!" Safe motion.
