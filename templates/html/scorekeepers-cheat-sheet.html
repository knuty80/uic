League Quick Reference

Prepare. Track. Respect.

Pre-Game & Lineups

  • Lineup Cards: Must be provided to the plate umpire and opposing scorekeeper 5 minutes prior to first pitch. Include First/Last Name, Number, and starting position.
  • Mandatory Play: Every rostered player must play at least 2 consecutive innings in the field and bat at least once. Substitutions must be reported to the umpire.
  • Time Limits: No new inning will start after {{ time_limit }}. If time expires during an incomplete inning, the score automatically rolls back to the end of the last fully completed inning.

Pitching Limitations & Rest

Scorekeepers must track pitches and alert the head coach when a pitcher is within 15 pitches of their limit. Accuracy is critical: exact pitch counts dictate required rest days and protect player arms. Violations result in Head Coach suspension and game forfeiture! A pitcher may finish the current batter if the limit is reached during an at-bat.

GameChanger Essentials

  • Pre-Game Setup: Input the opposing team's official lineup card before the first pitch. Verify both starting pitchers are accurately set.
  • Pitch Count Alerts: GameChanger tracks pitches, but you must verbally alert the coach when a pitcher is 15 pitches away from their limit.
  • Substitutions: Log defensive and offensive substitutions immediately as they are reported to the umpire to maintain accurate mandatory play records.
  • The "Undo" Button: If a play is entered incorrectly, use the Undo button immediately rather than trying to fix it later. Accuracy takes priority over speed.

Umpire Interaction & Conduct

The Golden Rule Only the Head Coach may approach the umpire to discuss a call. Assistant coaches and scorekeepers must direct questions through the Head Coach.
  • Judgment Calls Balls/Strikes, Safe/Out, and Fair/Foul are judgment calls. They are NOT arguable. Arguing judgment calls will result in an immediate warning.
  • Rule Interpretations If you believe a rule was applied incorrectly, request "Time". Once granted, approach the umpire respectfully to discuss the rule application.
  • Dugout Control The Head Coach is entirely responsible for the behavior of their players, assistant coaches, and parents in the stands.
